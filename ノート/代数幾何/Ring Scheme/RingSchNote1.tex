Let \(S\) be a scheme,
\(p:A\to S\) a ring scheme over \(S\).
Write \(m:A\times_S A \to A\) for the multiplication morphism of \(A\),
\(1_S:S\to A\) for the identity section of the multiplication, and
\(p_1:A\times_S A \to A\) for the first projection.
For any ring \(R\),
we shall write
\(R^{\times}\dfn \left\{ r\in R\middle| \text{\(r\) is invertible}\right\}\).
In this note, we prove that
if \(p:A\to S\) is flat and of finite presentation,
then the functor \(\Hom_S(-,A)^{\times}\)
is representable by an open subscheme of \(A\).


It is clearly that the following lemma holds:

\begin{lem}\label{lem: grp rep}
  The functor
  \begin{align*}
    \Hom_S(-,A)^{\times}:&\Sch{S} \to \mathsf{Grp} \\
    &T \mapsto \Hom_S(T,A)^{\times}
  \end{align*}
  is representable
  by \(A^{\times} \dfn (A\times_S A)\times_{m,A,1_S}A\).
\end{lem}

Write \(i:A^{\times}\to A\times_S A\) for the first projection.
Then, since \(\Hom_S(T,A)^{\times}\) is a subset of \(\Hom_S(T,A)\),
the following assertions hold:

\begin{lem}\label{lem: grp mono}
  The morphism
  \(p_1\circ i:A^{\times} \to A\)
  is a monomorphism of schemes.
\end{lem}


For any \(S\)-scheme \(X\) and any \(S\)-scheme \(T\),
we shall write \(X(T)\dfn \Hom_S(T,X)\) for
the set of \(T\)-valued points. 
The following lemma follows from elementary scheme theory:

\begin{lem}\label{lem: sq nil}
  Let
  \[
  \begin{CD}
    T_0 @>{t_0}>> X \\
    @V{i}VV @VVV \\
    T @>>> S
  \end{CD}
  \]
  be a commutative diagram of schemes
  such that \(i\) is a square nilpotent closed immersion,
  i.e., the ideal sheaf \(\mcI\) of \(i\) satisfies \(\mcI^2 = 0\).
  Then
\end{lem}



\begin{prop}\label{prop: grp open}
  Assume that \(p:A\to S\) is flat and of finite presentation.
  Then the morphism \(p_1\circ i:A^{\times} \to A\) is an open immersion.
\end{prop}

\begin{proof}
  By \autoref{lem: grp mono}, to prove \autoref{prop: grp open},
  it suffices to prove that \(p_1\circ i\) is smooth.
  Let \(a:T\to A\) be a morphism of \(S\)-schemes,
  \(j:T_0\to T\) a square nilpotent closed immersion, and
  \(a_0:T_0\to A^{\times}\) a morphism of \(A\)-schemes:
  \[
  \begin{CD}
    T_0 @>{a_0}>> A^{\times} \\
    @V{j}VV @VV{p_1\circ i}V \\
    T @>{a}>> A.
  \end{CD}
  \]

\end{proof}
